Tuscan Kale Soup

★★★

Servings: 4

Ingredients

1.5 tablespoons olive oil or garlic-infused olive oil

0.5 small white onion, chopped

2 celery sticks, chopped

1 carrot, chopped

0.5 teaspoon salt, plus more as needed

1 teaspoon dried oregano

0.5 teaspoon dried basil

0.5 teaspoon dried thyme

Pinch crushed red pepper flakes, more or less depending on spice preference

One 400 gram can diced tomatoes

480 ml Biome Broth (separate recipe)

90 grams uncooked quinoa

1 bay leaf

2 large handfuls chopped and cleaned kale

Half a 400 gram can of white beans, such as cannellini beans, navy beans, or butter beans, drained and rinsed

0.25 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more as needed

Description

This one-pot soup is thick and oh so satisfying, perfect for weeknights.

Directions

Heat the olive oil over medium heat in a medium or large pot or casserole. Add the onion, celery, carrot, and salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 3 to 5 minutes, until the onion becomes translucent and the vegetables begin to soften.

Add the oregano, basil, thyme, red pepper flakes, and tomatoes with their juice. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 1 to 2 minutes, until
well combined.

Add the broth, 240 ml water, the quinoa and bay leaf. Raise the heat up to medium-high, then bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes to allow the flavours to develop.

Uncover, stir in the kale and beans, and cook for about 5 minutes, and more salt, as needed, and serve until the greens are just wilted. Remove bay leaf, season with pepper and more salt, as needed, and serve.
