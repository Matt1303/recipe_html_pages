Coconut Curry Bowl

★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled Cookbook, Low FODMAP

Servings: 4

Ingredients

400 g extra-firm tofu, drained, and pressed

3 teaspoons olive oil

1 teaspoon ground turmeric

25 g thinly sliced green spring onion tops

1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger

140g torn kale leaves, stems removed

0.25 to 0.5 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

0.25 teaspoon chilli powder (garlic-free for low FODMAP)

1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon low-sodium tamari or soy sauce

2 teaspoons curry powder

240 ml canned coconut milk

480 ml Low FODMAP Biome Stock or other low FODMAP vegetable stock

2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice

0.5 teaspoon arrowroot powder (optional)

225g rice noodles


Supercharge It! (optional toppings): Jalapeño pepper seeded and diced Chopped fresh coriander leaves

Sesame seeds

Description

In a study of more than two thousand recipes, scientists discovered that the secret behind curry's popularity is the diverse mix of spices that have very different flavour profiles. Effectively, this creates an explosion of flavour that tickles the taste buds. Diversity of spices for the win!

FODMAP note: Many prepared veggie stocks contain garlic and onion, as well as other high FODMAPs like celery. Since it's hard to know the serving size of each vegetable included, we recommend a vegetable stock that's garlic-free and onion-free, or specifically low FODMAP. The Low FODMAP Biome Stock (page 85) is designed to serve this purpose.

Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.

2. Dice the tofu and toss with 1 teaspoon of the olive oil, 1/2 teaspoon of the turmeric, the black pepper, chilli powder, 1 tablespoon of the tamari, 1 teaspoon of the curry powder, and the arrowroot powder, if using. Place in a single layer on a rimmed baking tray and roast for 15 minutes, turning halfway through the cooking.

3. Soften the noodles according to the packet directions while the tofu cooks, then set aside.

4. In a medium saucepan, heat the remaining 2 teaspoons olive oil over medium heat and add the spring onion and ginger. Stir, cooking. for 30 seconds, until just fragrant. Add in the torn kale leaves and cook another 30 seconds then add in the remaining teaspoon of tamari, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric, and 1 teaspoon of curry powder along with the coconut milk and stock.

5. Bring to a simmer and let cook for 10 minutes. Remove from the heat and stir
in the lime juice.

6. Divide the tofu and noodles among four bowls. Top with coconut curry stock and optional toppings.

Notes

PRO TIP: Turmeric contains a phytochemical called curcumin that has tremendous anti-inflammatory properties. If you add black pepper to turmeric, as we do in this recipe, you can increase the absorption of curcumin by 2,000 per cent. That's not a typo.

FF UNLEASHED: If you are not following a low FODMAP approach, you can add 2 crushed garlic cloves with the onions and substitute 75g chopped white or yellow onions for the spring You can also add more canned coconut milk.
