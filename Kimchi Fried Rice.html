Kimchi Fried Rice

★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 2

Ingredients

450 grams kimchi

2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil, plus more if needed

3 spring onions, sliced

225 grams firm tofu, drained and pressed

3 garlic cloves, finely chopped

1 tablespoon fresh grated ginger root

2 bunches baby pak choi, thinly sliced

115 grams shiitake mushrooms, thinly sliced

1 tablespoon tamari, plus more if needed

400 grams cooked and cooled brown rice, reserved from Gumbo recipe

2 teaspoons rice wine vinegar

Description

This fried rice gets a Fibre Fuelled boost from the addition of kimchi, a Korean staple of fermented cabbage and vegetables.

Directions

Drain the kimchi in a colander, reserving the liquid. Roughly chop the kimchi a few times into bite-size pieces, then set aside.

Heat the sesame oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the spring onions and cook, stirring frequently, for 2 to 3 minutes, until tender. Crumble in the tofu and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 2 to 3 minutes, until lightly browned. Stir in the garlic and ginger.

Add in the pak choi and mushrooms and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes, until the pak choi is bright green and tender.

Add in the kimchi and tamari and cook until just heated through. Add in the rice, 1 tablespoon reserved kimchi brine, and the vinegar and cook, stirring frequently, for 3 to 4 minutes, until the mixture is heated through. Season to taste, adding more tamari, toasted sesame oil, or kimchi brine, as desired. Serve immediately.

Notes

Low FODMAP Options:

Kimchi is high in fructans. Many kimchi brands contain garlic, so if you are sensitive to fructans, then you can either omit the kimchi altogether or select a different recipe for dinner tonight.
Use only the green parts of spring onions for low FODMAP.
Garlic is high FODMAP and fructan. Omit and use 2 teaspoons garlic-infused olive oil instead.
A 70 gram serving of pak choi is FODMAP friendly. Over 100 grams per serving is moderate in sorbitol.
Shiitake mushrooms are high in mannitol. Substitute oyster mushrooms or omit.

Supercharge lt!
Top with sliced nori strips, sesame seeds, sliced spring onions, or pickled ginger.
