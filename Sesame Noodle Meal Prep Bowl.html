Sesame Noodle Meal Prep Bowl

★★★

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4 bowls

Ingredients

Sesame Dressing


60 grams tahini paste

2 tablespoons warm water

1 tablespoon tamari

2 teaspoons toasted sesamne oil

Juice of 1 lime

0.5 teaspoon garlic, finely chopped

0.5 teaspoon 100% maple syrup

0.25 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional)


Sesame Noodle Bowls


225 grams soba noodles

300 grams frozen shelled edamame, thawed

2 medium carrots, sliced very thin

240 grams diced cucumber

1 recipe Crispy Baked Tofu (separate recipe)

2 tablespoons sesame seeds, to serve

2 tablespoons hemp seeds, to serve

Description

These cold, Asian-inspired noodles are part of the Sunday meal-prep routine to be used for lunch later in the week. But if you start drooling when you prep them, we won't hold it against you if you give them a little taste.

Directions

Make the dressing: Whisk together the tahini, water, tamari, sesame oil, lime juice, garlic, maple syrup, and red pepper flakes until smooth. Set aside.

Cook the soba noodles according to packet instructions, then drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Return to the pan they were cooked in and toss with half of the prepared sesame sauce.

Divide the noodles among four resealable containers, then top with the edamame, carrots, cucumber, and tofu. Drizzle with the remaining sauce, the sesame seeds, and hemp seeds. Serve cold.

Notes

Lower FODMAP Options:

You can substitute the tahini with peanut butter or almond butter.
Garlic is high in fructans, replace with 1 teaspoon chives.

Supercharge It!

Sweet pepper, broccoli, and sliced spring onions would all taste great in this dish.
