Back-Pocket Stir-Fry

★★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4

Ingredients

80 ml tamari

60 ml rice wine vinegar

2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil

2 teaspoons cornflour or arrowroot

60 ml Biome Broth (separate recipe) or water

2 tablespoons freshly grated ginger root

1 red chilli, seeded and minced finely chopped (optional)

4 spring onions (green parts only), sliced

140 grams broccoli florets only, chopped

4 carrots, sliced on the diagonal

1 sweet red bell pepper, sliced

140 grams pak choi, sliced, leaves and stems separated

225 grams oyster mushrooms, sliced

70 grams cooked quinoa, to serve

Description

Simplicity wins here. This stir-fry is made for Week 1 but simple enough to mix and match with other vegetables after the
first week. Instead of a traditional protein source, we're serving this over quinoa, a pseudo-grain that packs in 8 grams of protein and 5 grams of fibre per cooked serving of 185 grams. Enjoy leftoves
for lunch later in the week.

Directions

In a small bowl, whisk together the tamari, vinegar, sesame oil, and cornflour until smooth. Set aside.

Heat the broth in a large frying pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the ginger, chilli, if using, and sliced spring onions. Cook, stirring often, until fragrant, about 1 minute.

Add the broccoli, carrots, sweet pepper, and pak choi stems and cook, stirring often, for 5 to 7 minutes, until the vegetables are bright in colour and slightly tender. Add in the pak choi leaves and oyster mushrooms and stir together for 30 seconds.

Add the tamari mixture to the wok. Cook, stirring often, until the sauce has thickened and the vegetables are cooked through.

Serve over cooked quinoa.

Notes

Low FODMAP Option:

After Week 2, add 1 or 2 crushed garlic cloves to the tamari mixture.
