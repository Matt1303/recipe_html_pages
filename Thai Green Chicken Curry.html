Thai Green Chicken Curry

Mob

Prep Time: 5 min Cook Time: 20 min Servings: Serves 4 Source: Mob.co.uk

Ingredients

2 Lemongrass Stalks

20 g Thai Basil

400 ml Coconut Milk

450 g Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs

½ Aubergine

1 Shallot

50 g Thai Green Curry Paste

250 ml Low Salt Chicken Stock

6 Baby Corn

2 tbsp Palm Sugar

2 tbsp Fish Sauce

4 Lime Leaves

400 g Bamboo Shoots

Vegetable Oil


To Serve:

320 g Jasmine Rice

Handful Fresh Coriander

1 Lime

1 Red Chilli

Handful Crispy Shallot

Description

A Thai green curry is hard to beat. This one is spicy, aromatic and coconutty, and quick and easy enough to make for dinner tonight. Perfect served over fluffy jasmine rice.

Directions

Bash the lemongrass and remove the bottom stems and outer husks. Cook the rice following packet instructions, and add the lemongrass stems and husks to the pot as it cooks.

Meanwhile, reserve a few Thai basil leaves for garnish and add the rest to a blender along with ¼ of the coconut milk. Blitz and set aside.

Slice the boneless chicken thighs into 1 inch strips. Dice the aubergine into bite-sized chunks. Peel and quarter the shallots. Finely slice the remaining lemongrass.

Heat a glug of vegetable oil in a medium-sized wok or pot set over a medium-high heat. Add the aubergine, a pinch of salt and ¼ of the remaining coconut milk and cook for 3 mins or so, stirring, until the coconut milk has thickened and turned lightly golden at the edges.

Add the green curry paste, lemongrass, shallot and remaining coconut milk to the pan, mix to combine then cook off for a minute or so. Add the chicken and cook for another 2 mins until the coconut milk has started to split and lightly caramelise around the edges.

Add the chicken stock and scrape down the edges of the pan. Then add the Thai basil coconut milk, baby corn, palm sugar, ½ the fish sauce, lime leaves, and a pinch of salt. Reduce the heat to low-medium and leave to simmer for 10 mins.

Drain and rinse the bamboo shoots then add to the pan and simmer for another 5 mins. Turn the heat off, stir through the remaining Thai basil leaves and leave to rest with the lid on for 5 mins. Taste and season with more salt and fish sauce if necessary.

Meanwhile, pick the coriander leaves, cut the lime into wedges and thinly slice the red chilli for garnish.

To serve, divide the rice between bowls and ladle over the curry. Serve with the coriander, lime and fresh chilli.
