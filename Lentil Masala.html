Lentil Masala

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Servings: 2

Ingredients

Coriander-Lime Rice


95 grams uncooked basmati or brown rice

Salt

2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice

0.25 cup finely chopped coriander


Lentils


2 teaspoons olive oil or Biome Broth

0.5 yellow or white onion, chopped

2 cm piece fresh ginger root, grated or finely chopped

2 garlic cloves, finely chopped

Pinch salt and freshly ground black pepper

teaspoon garam masala

0.5 teaspoon chilli powder

0.5 teaspoon ground cinnamon

2 tablespoons tomato pasta

95 grams dried green or brown lentils


Cashew Cream


30 grams raw cashews (20 whole cashews)

80 ml warm water


Freshly squeezed lime juice to serve

Fresh coriander, to serve

Description

It's no secret that we are big fans of Indian food. Packed with fragrant, antioxidant-rich spices, aromatics vegetables, and pulses. Indian cuisine is a great way to enjoy traditional Fibre Fuelled cuisine.

This simplified lentil masala uses cashew cream, a delicious, dairy-free alternative that tastes just as rich and luscious. If you haven't made cashew cream before, this is one recipe that you'll likely turn to over and over again; it works well in any recipe that calls for heavy cream.

Directions

Make the rice: In a small saucepan, bring 240 ml of cold water to a boil over medium-high heat. Add the rice, reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook according to the packet instructions, until the rice is tender. Stir in 1/4 teaspoon salt, 1 tablespoon of the lime juice, and the coriander. Set aside.

Make the lentils: Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add in the onion, ginger, and garlic and cook for 5 to 7 minutes, until softened. Add the salt and pepper along with the garam masala, chilli powder, and cinnamon. Cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.

Add the tomato paste and cook for 1 more minute, until dark red and fragrant. Add the lentils and 480 ml cold water, then bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover, and cook for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are tender and most of the water has been absorbed.

Make the cashew cream: Combine the cashews and warm water in a blender and blend until very creamy and thick. Depending on the power of your blender, this may take up to 5 minutes.

Stir the cashew cream and remaining 1 tablespoon lime juice into the lentils and season to taste, adding more salt as needed. Serve over the reserved coriander-lime rice, topping with more coriander and lime juice as desired.
