Lentil-Walnut Tacos / Sweet Potatoes

★★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Servings: Makes 4 servings, with enough for leftovers for Stuffed Taco Sweet Potatoes

Ingredients

Sweet Potatoes


60 grams walnuts, finely chopped

1 tablespoon olive oil or Biome Broth

1 large tomato, chopped

Two 400 gram cans brown lentils, drained and rinsed

2 teaspoons dried oregano

2 teaspoons ground cumin

2 tablespoons chilli powder

0.5 teaspoon salt

0.25 cup water


Tacos


4 to 6 corn tortillas

Half recipe Lentil-Walnut Filling

Creamy Coriander Sauce (separate recipe), to serve

Diced tomatoes, chopped fresh coriander, shredded lettuce, and/or sliced black olives, to serve


Stuffed Taco Sweet Potatoes


2 medium sweet potatoes

Half recipe Lentil-Walnut Filling

Diced tomatoes, chopped fresh coriander, shredded lettuce, and or sliced black olives, to serve

Creamy Coriander Sauce (separate recipe)

Description

You'll use this lentil walnut taco filling in both the Lentil-Walnut Tacos and the Stuffed Taco Sweet Potatoes. We love recipes that do double duty and save you time in the kitchen.

Directions

Heat a large frying pan over medium heat and add the walnuts. Toast, stirring often, for about 2 minutes, until the walnuts are lightly browned and fragrant, taking care not to burn. Remove the walnuts from the pan and set aside.

Add the oil and tomato to the same frying pan and cook until the tomato is soft, 3 to 4 more minutes. Add the canned lentils, oregano, cumin, chilli powder, salt, and water. Cook until heated through, gently mashing some of the lentils with the back of a wooden spoon or spatula, for 5 minutes.

Taste, adding more chilli powder or salt, if desired.


To make the tacos:
Warm the corn tortillas and top with the
filling and the toppings of your choice.

To make the stuffed sweet potatoes: Preheat the oven to 200°C.
Using a fork, poke the sweet potatoes a few times on each side to help release steam as they cook. Place directly into the oven and cook until tender, 45 to 55 minutes for smaller sweet potatoes, 55 to 70 minutes for larger ones.

Remove the potatoes from the oven and split them down the middle. Top with the Lentil-Walnut Filling, Creamy Coriander
Sauce, and desired toppings.
