Miso, Mushroom, and Soba Bowl

★★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 2

Ingredients

75 grams frozen, shelled edamame

115 grams uncooked soba noodles

10 to 20 mangetout, vertically sliced

2 teaspoons sesame oil

60 ml Biome Broth or vegetable stock

1 teaspoon miso paste

0.25 teaspoon 100% maple syrup

1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger root

0.25 teaspoon salt; plus more to taste

1 teaspoon garlic-infused olive oil

90 grams thinly sliced red cabbage

4 rehydrated dried shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and sliced

1 to 2 carrots, shaved into ribbons with vegetable peeler (1 large or 2 small)

4 spring onions, sliced

Description

"Soba" is the Japanese word for "buckwheat," which is the wheat-free flour that makes up soba noodles. Oishi is the Japanese word for "delicious," which is what you'll be saying while you enjoy the soba.

Directions

Bring a medium saucepan of water to the boil over medium-high heat. Add the edamame and soba noodles and cook for 2 minutes.

Add in the sliced mangetout and cook for an additional minute, until the noodles are tender. Drain and rinse with cold water to stop cooking. Set aside.

In a small bowl, whisk together the sesame oil, stock, miso paste, maple syrup, ginger root, and salt. Set aside.

Heat the olive oil in a medium frying pan over medium heat, then add in the cabbage, mushrooms, carrots, and spring onions. Toss and cook for about 3 minutes, until just tender.

Add the edamame, noodles, mangetout, and sesame sauce to the frying pan and toss until the dish is heated through, 2 to 3 minutes.

Notes

Supercharge It!

Add a serving of cooked tofu for a higher protein dish.
