Plant-Powered Polenta Ragu

★★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 2

Ingredients

Quick Ragu


2 tespoons olive oil, garlic-infused olive oil, or vegetable stock

200 grams chopped tomatoes

Salt and freshly ground black pepper

200 grams tinned lentils

140 grams Roasted Italian Medley (separate recipe)

0.5 teaspoon dried oregano, plus more to taste

0.5 teaspoon dried basil, plus more to taste

Crushed red pepper flakes (optional)



Polenta



360 ml plain almond milk

80 grams cornmeal

0.5 teaspoon salt

Freshly ground black pepper



Chopped parsley, to serve

Chopped basil, to serve

Description

Creamy polenta is stick-to-your-ribs comfort food, especially when combined with our quick ragu of Italian vegetables and lentils. After Week 1, try this ragu with Pumpkin Seed Parmesan (page 300),a nutty parm that's delicious on everything from pasta to polenta to popcorn.

Directions

Make the ragu: Heat a medium saucepan over medium heat and add the olive oil. Add in the tomatoes and a pinch of salt and black pepper. Cook, stirring often, for about 10 minutes, until the tomatoes have broken down. Add the lentils, Roasted Italian Medley, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes, if using. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes, or until thickened. Taste as you go and add more salt, pepper, and dried herbs as desired

Make the polenta: Whisk together 120 ml water and the almond milk in a medium saucepan over medium heat. When bubbles start to surface, whisk in the cornmeal and salt. Reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 10 to 15 minutes, until thickened. Taste as you go and add more salt and pepper to taste.

To serve, divide the polenta into two bowls and top with the ragu. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and basil.
