Banana Baked Oatmeal

★★★★

Breakfasts, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4

Ingredients

1 large banana, sliced

150 grams quick-cooking oats

2 tablespoons ground flaxseeds

0.5 teaspoon ground cinnamon

1 teaspoon baking powder

0.25 teaspoon salt

180 ml almond milk or other dairy-free milk

80 ml 100% maple syrup

2 tablespoons organic sunflower oil

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

30 grams chopped walnuts or other chopped nut

Description

Baked oatmeal is one of our favourite warm breakfasts. While this one is delicious right out of the pan, we recommend enjoying it warm, topped with cold almond milk, chopped nuts, and extra berries.

To prepare ahead, assemble everything the night before to bake in the morning or prep on Sunday and reheat with a little more
almond milk as it will dry out slightly in the fridge.

Directions

Preheat the oven to 175°C. Lightly spray a 20 x 20 cm baking dish.

Place the sliced bananas in a single row on the bottom of the dish and set aside

In a medium bowl, whisk together the oats, flaxseeds, cinnamon baking powder, and salt.

In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the almond milk maple syrup, sunflower oil, and vanilla. Add to the oat mixture and
stir together until combined. Stir in the chopped nuts and gently top the sliced bananas in the baking dish with the oatmeal. Place in the oven and bake for 30 minutes, or until golden brown and set.

Notes

Low FODMAP Options:
Quick-cooking oats have moderate GOS and fructans. Substitute with jumbo oats.

Supercharge It!
Top with berries, almond milk, and chopped nuts.
