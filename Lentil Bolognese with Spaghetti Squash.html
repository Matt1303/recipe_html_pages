Lentil Bolognese with Spaghetti Squash

★★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 2

Ingredients

1 large spaghetti squash, halved and seeds scooped out

1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon olive oil

0.75 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste

0.25 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste

1 medium shallot, finely chopped

4 garlic cloves, finely chopped

2 medium carrots, chopped

2 celery sticks, chopped

1 litre tomato sauce

150 grams dry red lentils

1 teaspoon dried basil

1 teaspoon dried oregano

1 pinch crushed red pepper flakes

Pumpkin Seed Parmesan (separate recipe), to serve

Description

If you can't find spaghetti squash, no worries! Just substitute cooked pasta of choice. Boom. Ya good.

Directions

Preheat the oven to 200°C.

Rub the squash with 1 teaspoon olive oil and season with 0.25 teaspoon salt and 0.25 teaspoon pepper. Place the squash cut-side down on a rimmed baking tray and roast for 45 to 50 minutes, until tender. Remove and let cool until able to touch, then use a fork to scrape out the spaghetti-like strands. Set aside.

Make the sauce: Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large rimmed frying pan over medium heat and add the shallot and garlic. Sauté, stirring frequently, for about 3 minutes, until soft and fragrant, taking care not to burn.

Add the carrots and celery and cook for 4 to 5 minutes, until the vegetables are softened. Add the tomato sauce, lentils, basil, oregano, red pepper flakes, and 120 ml water. Raise the heat to bring the mixture to a low boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are tender. If the mixture is cooking too quickly, add more water to prevent it from drying out. Add in the remaining 0.5 teaspoon of salt. Add more salt and pepper to taste.

Divide the spaghetti squash strands between two bowls and top with the lentil Bolognese and additional garnishes, if using.

Notes

Low FODMAP Option:

Shallots are high in fructans. Omit and use shallot-infused olive oil (either use store-bought or make your own) in place of the regular olive oil, or swap in 25 grams spring onion greens.
Garlic is high in FODMAP and fructans. Omit and use 25 grams chives, or substitute garlic-infused olive oil (see page 246) in place of the plain olive oil.
Celery is high FODMAP. Reduce to 0.67 medium celery stick.
Tomato sauce may contain onion and garlic. Look for brands that contain just tomato. If substituting with marinara sauce, then look for these low FODMAP options if you are sensitive to fructans.
Dry red lentils have moderate GOS. Substitute 200 grams canned lentils.


Supercharge It!

Top with the Pumpkin Seed Parmesan, chopped fresh basil, and chopped fresh parsley.
