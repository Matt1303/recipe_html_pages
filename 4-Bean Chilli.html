4-Bean Chilli

★★★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled, Lunches

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 6

Ingredients

1 tablespoon olive oil

1 white or yellow onion, chopped

1 yellow sweet pepper, chopped

2 garlic cloves, finely chopped

3 tablespoons chilli powder, plus more to taste

1 tablespoon ground cumin, Plus more to taste

2 teaspoons dried oregano, plus more to taste

480 ml low-sodium vegetable stock

240 ml tomato sauce

One 400 gram can diced tomatoes

One 400 gram can black beans

One 400 gram can kidney beans

One 400 gram can pinto beans

One 400 gram can cannellini beans

0.25 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste

Description

You'll enjoy this Bean Chilli for dinner one night, then as Chilli Mac later in the week. For a heartier meal, serve over cooked grains of choice, like quinoa. This recipe contains many high FODMAP ingredients, so substitute Butternut Squash and Quinoa Chilli (page 266) if necessary.

Directions

Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat until shimmering. Add the onion, sweet pepper, and garlic and cook for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is translucent. Add the chilli powder, cumin, and oregano and cook for 30 to 60 seconds, until fragrant.

Add the stock, tomato sauce, tomatoes, black beans, kidney beans, pinto beans, cannellini beans, and salt and raise the heat to medium-high to bring to a boil, Reduce the heat to low and simmer uncovered
for 30 to 45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is thick and the flavours have developed. Season to taste, as desired.

Serve, reserving 600 grams of the chilli to make Chilli Mac (separate recipe), or leftovers.

Notes

Low FODMAP Options:
Tomato sauce may contain onion and garlic. If you are sensitive to fructans, look for low FODMAP tomato sauce options
Cashews are high in GOS and have moderate fructans. You can substitute the 30 grams cashews with 55 grams walnuts. Soak the walnuts overnight before using, then make according to the recipe above.
If you're sensitive to fructans in the wheat in the macaroni noodles, substitute quinoa or rice-based pasta.
Black beans are high in GOS and have moderate fructans.
Kidney beans are high in GOS and fructans.
Pinto beans are high in GOS and fructans.
Cannellini beans are high FODMAP.


Meal Prep Tip:
Make this chilli on Sunday to enjoy for dinner on Sunday and lunch on Thursday, with the option for Chilli Mac instead of Chilli that day.

Supercharge It!
Top with sliced spring onions, chopped fresh coriander, and sliced jalapeños.
