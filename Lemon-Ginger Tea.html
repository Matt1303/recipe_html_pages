Lemon-Ginger Tea

Drinks, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 2

Ingredients

1 small ginger root, sliced into four 2 cm chunks

Juice of 1 large lemon

100% maple syrup or stevia, as desired

Description

We like this lemon-ginger tea as an after-dinner drink. Ginger aids in digestion, and lemon contains just enough pucker to satisfy any dessert cravings. Feel free to drink it liberally, whenever you feel it
suits you.

Directions

In a medium saucepan, simmer 1 litre of water and the ginger root chunks for 10 to 15 minutes, or longer, depending on how strong you like your tea.

Remove from the heat and stir in the lemon juice. Strain, removing the ginger, and divide the tea between 2 large mugs. Stir in
the maple syrup and serve.

To serve cold: Pour the ginger-water mixture over 2 large glasses of ice and add the lemon juice and maple syrup, as desired.
