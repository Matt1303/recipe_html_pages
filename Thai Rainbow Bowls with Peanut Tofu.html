Thai Rainbow Bowls with Peanut Tofu

★★★

Dinner, Fibre Fuelled

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 2

Ingredients

95 grams uncooked brown

170 grams firm or extra-firm tofu, drained, pressed, and cubed.


Spicy Peanut Sauce


80 grams creamy peanut butter

1 tablespoon tamari

1 tablespoon 100% maple syrup

3 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice

0.25 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes, or more or less for spice preference

1 garlic clove, finely chopped

2 tablespoons peanuts, finely chopped, plus more for garnish

3 tablespoons hot water

Salt and freshly ground black pepper

1 carrot, shaved into long, noodle-like strips with a vegetable peeler

120 grams (half-moon shapes) cucumber

70 grams green or red cabbage

0.5 red sweet pepper, sliced

2 spring onions, chopped

Description

dish. If you notice symptoms coming back, make sure to check out the lower FODMAP options below.

Directions

Preheat the oven to 200°C.

Bring 240 ml to a boil in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the rice, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Simmer until the rice is tender, about 30 minutes then lightly fluff with a fork and
set aside.

Place the tofu in a single layer on a rimmed non-stick baking tray and cook for 25 minutes, or until lightly browned. If you aren't using a non-stick baking sheet, lightly spray with cooking spray. Remove the tofu from the oven, place in a shallow bowl, and set aside.

Make the sauce: Whisk together the peanut butter, tamari, maple syrup, lime juice, red pepper flakes, garlic, and peanuts until creamy and thick. Keep whisking, slowly whisking in hot water until the sauce is pourable. Season to taste with salt and pepper, then add 3 tablespoons of the sauce to the cooked tofu and toss to coat. Set aside.

Divide the cooked rice into two bowls and top with the tofu, carrots, cucumbers, cabbage, sweet peppers, and spring onions. Drizzle with the remaining Spicy Peanut Sauce and serve.

Notes

Make-Ahead Tips:

The tofu can be baked abead of time and stored in an airtight container in the fridge. It won't be crispy after being stored in the fridge, so you can fry it in a lightly oiled frying pan before serving, if desired The Spicy Peanut Sautce can be made ahead of time; store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days.

Low FODMAP Options:

Garlic is high in fructans. Omit and replace with teaspoon sliced spring onion greens.
Red, Savoy, and common cabbage are moderate FODMAP when you use more than 50 grams per serving.
The white bulb of the spring onion is high in fructans, so only use the green parts for low FODMAP.
